Short CV

Here’s a brief overview of my professional journey so far. For each job, I have summarized what I did. If you’re interested in what I learned doing these jobs, I’ve written up some of my learnings in the long bio.

Scientific Collaborator @ Global Health Engineering #

Apr 2024 – present | Switzerland

At GHE, I write papers and grants in various fields, teach students what it means to be an international engineer, and conduct research on maize fertilizer in Malawi. I also support team members with data management, statistical analysis, and the computational reproducibility of their work.

Senior Data Officer @ IMPACT Initiatives #

Apr 2022 – Oct 2023 | Nigeria & Mali

At IMPACT, I was responsible for the data pipelines behind multi-sectoral needs assessments (Nigeria in 2022 and Mali in 2023). This meant I developed sampling strategies, programmed surveys (with Kobo), monitored data quality during the assessments, and supervised my team’s analysis of the results.

Research Assistant @ Swiss Environmental Panel #

Sep 2019 - Apr 2022 | Switzerland

At the SEP, I set up all the surveys (in Qualtrics) and developed a software package (sep) to automate data visualizations for our reports.

Pizzaiolo @ Palazzo Margherita #

Jul 2019 - Aug 2019 | Italy

In the summer of 2019, I had the amazing opportunity to learn the craft of pizzamaking in Southern Italy.

Staff @ Cuisine sans frontières #

Oct 2018 - Apr 2019 | Ecuador

While working for CSF in Ecuador, I helped organize gastronomy courses and relaunch a chocolate cooperative together with a Quechua community.

Research Assistant @ University of Geneva #

Feb 2017 - Feb 2022 | Switzerland

Working as a research assistant for Prof. Thomas Sattler, I helped him program and analyze online surveys to better understand the political economy behind austerity.

Education #

Comparative and International Studies @ ETH Zurich #

A fancy name for a political science program with a focus on research and quantitative methods. I really liked it.

International Relations (BARI) @ University of Geneva #

A mix of history, political science, economics, and law. Living in Geneva was great; the program itself, not so much.

Languages #

  • (Swiss) German: My mother tongue.
  • English: The foreign language I speak best. I use it every day for work, writing, and reading.
  • French: The foreign language I like speaking the most. I’ve been with the language for more than half my life, though I only truly learned it during my undergrad. I speak it fluently and wish I could use it every single day.
  • Italian: I’ve always had a passion for Italian but never really learned it. Then, in 2019, I had no choice and had to learn it for my apprenticeship in Italy and never looked back. I can have a conversation, but I wish I spoke it better.
  • Spanish: I learned it in high school and spoke it fluently while working in Ecuador, but unfortunately, it got a little rusty over the years. Whenever I listen to old salsa songs, I think I should practice it again.
